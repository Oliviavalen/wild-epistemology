#### A Discovery of Witches

All these examples are also related to A Discovery of Witches. In ADOW, daemons represent one-third of the supernatural factions in the All Souls universe. Their ranks commonly include:

#### Creative and Artistic Visionaries
* Artists and Sculptors: They possess a deep, almost obsessive drive to create visual art.
* Musicians and Composers: Many of history’s greatest musical geniuses were actually daemons.
* Writers and Poets: They use their intense emotional depth to craft literature.

#### Innovators, Inventors, and Tech Experts
* Mad Scientists: their minds process information in non-linear ways, leading to radical scientific breakthroughs that regular humans deem impossible.
* Tech Whizzes and Hackers: They easily master complex computer systems, coding, and digital networks.
* Engineers and Architects: They design intricate structures and systems ahead of their time.

#### High-Stakes Business and Finance
* Algos and Day Traders: They see patterns in numbers and stock markets that others miss.
* Entrepreneurs: Their erratic, high-energy genius helps them build massive business empires in industries like finance and law.

##### But the dark side of all that is mental instability and loneliness.
Because their minds operate at such a high frequency, and because they are uniquely condemned to lives of isolation, daemons are highly prone to mental illness, depression, and addiction. Without a creative or intellectual outlet, their genius often collapses into severe instability, which is why they are frequently misunderstood and under-represented by both humans and other creatures.

Famous figures who fit this archetype—like Steve Jobs, Elon Musk, Mozart, Christopher Marlowe, Nolan Ross, Bruce Wayne, and Sherlock Holmes—are rare examples of high-functioning daemons. Most are fated to short lives full of tragedy. You know the type.

So whether you go into abstraction, niffin out, fall into a 2-Dimensional World, lose touch with reality as an adult, or just feel like you've always been out of place—it's all the exact same thing.

That wasn't my point, though.
#
Ever since I discovered the existence of GitHub—which was about four days ago—I knew I was going to create a file for all my thought fragments and abstract thoughts. It takes me about two to five seconds to conceptualize, solve, and completely work through an idea in my head, but it takes a few days to write and edit it. By the time I've completed something suitable for public presentation, I've already solved a thousand other equations in my head and no longer care.

So I knew I wanted a place where I could write down all my thoughts without having to refine and organize them for other people. Just raw mental data processing available for anyone who dares brave the labyrinth of my mind. My son mentioned the term abstraction and I had my file title.
